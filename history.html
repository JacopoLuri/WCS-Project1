History

First electric cars

The beginning of the electric car dates back to the 19th century where the electric engine was invented in 1835 even before the gasoline engine in 1870 and in 1885 the first production electric car was made.

Golden time

In the beginning of the 20th century electric cars had some advantages over their competetitors. One of those things was the smell and noise which the petrol car suffered from. they also didn't suffer from the problem of long start-up times like the steam-powered cars and they also didn't need a manuel effort to start like a hand crank. they main problem for the electric car was the limited range.

In the United States around 1912 40% of the cars were powered by steam, 38% by electricity and 22% by gasoline.

Falldown

In the 1920s the falldown of the electric car started. One of the reasons is that the need for greater range couldn't be offered by electric cars. Worldwide large quantities of petroleum were discovered so gasoline became o lot more effortable. The problems gasoline cars struggled from like noise and the need of a hand crank were also fixed.
